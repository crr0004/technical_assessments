5 Questions About the Biden Vaccine Mandate

5 Questions About the Biden Vaccine Mandate

The Biden administration has released details of its plan to prevent the spread of the coronavirus at work. It requires 84 million people to get vaccinated by Jan. 4 or do weekly testing.

Here are answers to some of the questions readers submitted →

5 Questions About the Biden Vaccine Mandate

What is in OSHA’s new rule?

Private companies of 100 or more employees must require their workers to be fully vaccinated by Jan. 4 or submit to weekly coronavirus testing and wear masks while in the workplace.

The deadline for employers to enforce the mask mandate is Dec. 5.

All employees, including those working part time, count toward the 100-worker threshold. Independent contractors do not.

5 Questions About the Biden Vaccine Mandate

Does the new rule apply to remote workers?

Remote workers and people who work exclusively outdoors don’t have to get vaccinated or submit to weekly testing.

The Occupational Safety and Health Administration is focusing specifically on ensuring people’s protection against the coronavirus in the workplace.

Employees who work part of the time in the office and the rest of the time remotely are required to follow the rule.

5 Questions About the Biden Vaccine Mandate

Can employees opt for testing instead of getting vaccinated? Who pays for those tests?

It will be up to companies to determine whether their workers can opt out of getting vaccinated by submitting to testing. If workers choose to be tested instead of being vaccinated, they must also be masked.

OSHA does not require employers to pay for tests, given that the vaccine is free and highly effective. Businesses may be required to pay under collective bargaining agreements or local laws.

5 Questions About the Biden Vaccine Mandate

Will employees be paid for the time it takes to get vaccinated? What about for getting boosters?

Employers have to provide up to four hours of paid time off for their workers to get vaccinated, as well as sick leave for them to recover from side effects. They are required to provide this leave starting Dec. 5.

Booster shots aren’t currently required under OSHA’s rule, so employers most likely don’t have to provide paid time for workers to get them. They are also not required to provide leave for testing.

5 Questions About the Biden Vaccine Mandate

Can employers fire workers who don’t comply? If workers are fired for not following the rules, are they eligible for unemployment?

When it comes to people who do not have exemptions, employers are generally free to discipline people who don’t follow their rules.

A worker’s eligibility for unemployment is determined on a state-by-state basis. Typically, people qualify for unemployment if they’re terminated through no fault of their own, but each state has its own standards.
